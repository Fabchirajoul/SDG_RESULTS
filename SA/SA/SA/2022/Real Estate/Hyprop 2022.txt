Dear shareholders,

I am pleased to present Hyprop’s remuneration report for 2022. The year continued to be challenging for all our stakeholders as markets adapted

to post Covid-19 lockdowns, civil unrest, and an underperforming economy. During this period our employees have once again worked with

passion and dedication, responding positively to the difficult environment and striving to meet key objectives of the Group. This was particularly

true of those working at shopping centres where they continually engage with shoppers and tenants who themselves have been navigating

uncertain times, adding another dimension to an already challenging environment.

RemCo is responsible to the Board in ensuring that the remuneration policy and practices are aligned with Hyprop’s strategic objectives and are

fair and reasonable to all stakeholders. These policies and practices were reviewed during the year, resulting in a new Long Term Incentive Plan

(LTIP) approved by shareholders at a General Meeting on 20 July 2022. To further support the LTIP, the malus and clawback policy was reviewed

and a minimum shareholding requirement policy was introduced.

We wish to have a trusted and transparent relationship with our stakeholders and our remuneration structure is appropriately designed to be

performance based, linked to realistic performance objectives in support of the Group’s strategy. The structure is regularly reviewed to ensure

that it is representative of best practice in the industry.

It is important to regularly review and rotate the composition of RemCo to ensure that the committee collectively possess the required skills and

expertise. In this regard two new members have been appointed to RemCo, namely Spiros Noussis and Richard Inskip with Thabo Mokgatlha

stepping down to focus on his role as Chairman of the Audit and Risk committee.

Key decisions taken during 2022 and focus areas for 2023

The LTIP was finalised by RemCo for recommendation of approval by the Board and Hyprop shareholders. Salient details of the LTIP are

summarised in Part B of the Remuneration report. This will replace the previous long-term incentive plan (the CUP), which was approaching the

end of its life cycle, subject to pre-existing CUP awards continuing under the CUP rules.

The short- and long-term performance measurements and targets for 2023 awards were set in consideration of the LTIP and the strategic

objectives of the Group.

The malus and clawback policy was updated to include all participants who are granted incentive awards in accordance with the

remuneration policy.

A minimum shareholding requirement was introduced for executive directors and prescribed officers.

Remuneration consultants

During the year under review, Hyprop engaged the services of

PwC, Vasdex Associates and 21 Century Proprietary Limited on

remuneration matters, to assist RemCo in determining and ensuring

remuneration practices remain fair and transparent, competitive

and affordable. RemCo is satisfied that the remuneration

consultants engaged were independent and objective.

Shareholder engagement

The Remuneration report aims to provide shareholders with

sufficient information to make an assessment of the Group’s

remuneration policies and the implementation thereof, in order to

make an informed decision when voting on the remuneration related

resolutions proposed at the AGM, in terms of section 66(9) of the

Companies Act, 2008 and King IV™.

The following resolutions will be tabled for shareholder approval at

the AGM on 25 November 2022, details of which can be found in the

AGM notice which is available online at www.hyprop.co.za:

• Non-binding advisory vote on the remuneration policy

(Part B of this report);

• Non-binding advisory vote on the implementation report

(Part C of this report); and

• Binding vote on non-executive directors’ fees.

Should either of the non-binding resolutions receive dissenting votes

in excess of 25%, RemCo will:

• embark on formal engagements with dissenting shareholders

regarding their dissenting votes;

• address legitimate and reasonable objections raised; and

• if required, amend the remuneration policy or clarify and/or

adjust the remuneration governance, processes or disclosure.
